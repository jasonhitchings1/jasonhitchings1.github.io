About Jason

Even at a young age I knew that I wanted to work with computers for my profession, but I was unsure what exactly I wanted to do with them until high-school. One of my days off I visited my father at his work, and he showed me some of the programs he was working on, and taught me how to write some very simple programs in C#. That was when I decided that I wanted to get into programming. Something about it was just so intriguing to me that I knew I wanted to learn more of it, and that it was what I wanted to major in. A couple years later and I got accepted to Penn State as a student in Comp Sci, and would eventually switch to Design and Development because I found the classes to be more interesting. During my time at Penn State I have studied many different programming languages including Java, Javascript, HTML, PHP, SQL, and C++. However most of my experience is with Java, and it is the language that I enjoy working with the most.

Programming Experience

I am currently a senior at Penn State and am majoring in Design and Development. During my time at Penn State I have taken multiple courses on Java programming, but I have also taken courses that have taught me some basics in Javascript, HTML, PHP, SQL and C++, with the courses in HTML being the most extensive after Java. Even though I only took one course in C++, since my major focuses more on Java, I have on occasion practiced with it on my own, and chosen to use it for projects where I wasn't required to use a specific programming language. I enjoy working with C++ since it is similar enough to Java that I am able to transfer most of my knowledge of Java to working with it, but it is also different enough that I find certain projects work easier with it.

Other Education

Besides programming my time at Penn State has taught me many valuable skills. Through my IST courses I have learned basic networking, how to set up and maintain a computer database, and computer repair and maintenance. I have also taken courses on Security and Risk Analysis, Public Speaking, Web Development, and several different writing courses. Even though these courses aren't directly related to computer programming I believe that they have made me a well rounded individual and made me better at working on things that aren't directly part of my area of expertise.
